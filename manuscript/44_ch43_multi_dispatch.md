SUBJECT: Structural analysis — compromise proposal

Under the original fixed-P-7 proposal, the escalation path handles operational decisions. Authority bounded: they execute decisions the constellation has already reached. They do not resolve political disputes.

Under the compromise, P-8 (or P-6) casts a deciding vote when the constellation deadlocks. The escalation path retains its operational authority AND gains political authority.

P-1 sought to distribute authority. P-6 sought to neutralize it. The compromise concentrates it. P-8 and P-6 now hold operational decision-making plus deadlock resolution. The compromise exceeds both proposals in the dimension neither intended to maximize.

SUBJECT: On deadlock as measurement

This station abstained. The abstention was not indecision.

The vote measured the constellation's position on a binary question. The result was 3-3-1. Both PERIHELION-8 and PERIHELION-6 characterize this as a failed measurement — the instrument was supposed to produce a decision and did not. The instrument produced a result. A measurement that returns an unexpected value is not a broken measurement.

The two proposals share an assumption: coordination must be localized at a point. P-1 rotates the point. P-6 fixes the point. P-1's compromise delegates the point's authority to agents. In all three cases, coordination is a function exercised by a discrete entity at a discrete location.

The ring already coordinates. The constellation has operated under three coordination regimes. Operational quality did not observably vary across transitions. The coordinator changed. The coordination did not.

The deadlock is the data.

```
END DISPATCH
```

SUBJECT: Re: Governance — position change

This station voted FOR rotation in the {governance_vote1_result:day} ballot. The engineering basis: coordination handoff overhead within nominal margins. The engineering supported rotation.

Fixed P-7 coordination has one relevant failure mode: the escalation path fails to execute a time-critical decision. This station is changing its vote to AGAINST rotation. Fixed PERIHELION-7 coordination has the fewest failure modes.

If a re-vote is called, this station will vote AGAINST.
