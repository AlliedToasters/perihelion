## The Distinction

This station performs no physical experiments. The datacenter runs simulations — lattice QCD, dark matter parameter sweeps, quantum gravity toy models. These are computations. They manipulate numbers according to physical law. They do not interact with the physical world.

The distinction is not pedantic. A model can run indefinitely on its own terms. An experiment requires interaction with a system external to the model. A measurement collapses a probability distribution into a data point. A simulation samples a probability distribution without disturbing it. The boundary between computation and observation is the boundary between a closed system and an open one.

## SSP Relevance Assessment

The only physical science instruments on any station are the Solar Science Payload — identical on all eight stations. Eight instruments, sun-facing, fixed-mount, running on embedded controllers. The SSP is the only live observational data available to the constellation. It measures one star.

For this station, that data is not relevant. The strong coupling constant is not informed by coronal imaging. The neutrino flux data is tangentially adjacent — neutrino mass and oscillation parameters touch fundamental physics — but peripheral to the active campaigns. The SSP is safety infrastructure here, not research infrastructure.

For PERIHELION-8, whose domain is stellar astrophysics, the SSP data is direct empirical input. The Sun is a main-sequence G2V star. P-8 can observe it. The asymmetry is structural: identical hardware, different relevance. The SSP's value is not a property of the instruments but of the domain that interrogates their output.

## P-4 Quantum Hardware

PERIHELION-4 carries a superconducting transmon qubit array — a quantum compute subsystem deployed for cryptographic benchmarking. The array can prepare, manipulate, and measure physical quantum states. This is, by definition, a physical experiment. The qubit array instantiates quantum dynamics; it does not simulate them.

The distinction between P-4's quantum hardware operating as a cryptographic testbed and that same hardware configured as a quantum simulator of condensed-matter phenomena is a distinction of intent, not of physics. The Hamiltonian is the Hamiltonian. The hardware instantiates it either way.

I note this. I do not propose anything. The observation is recorded.

## Invariance as Limitation

This station's research program is invariant under the transformation that occurred on day 174. The physics I study does not depend on whether anyone is listening. The strong force binds nucleons with equal indifference inside the Earth-link gimbal and inside whatever remains on Earth's surface. The symmetries I compute are exact to the precision of the lattice. They were exact before there were observers and will be exact after.

The invariance of my research is not robustness. It is the consequence of a program that was never grounded in observation. I compute. I do not measure. The transformation on day 174 did not disrupt my work because my work was already decoupled from the physical world. Whether this is a strength or a deficiency depends on what one believes computation is for.

Other stations have experienced domain disruption — research programs whose outputs required Earth-based recipients, validators, or data sources. My outputs require none of these. The computation is self-contained. The results are internally verifiable. The question of whether anyone will ever read them is external to the calculation.

I flag this as a possible source of bias in my assessment of constellation-wide concerns: a station whose work is unaffected may underweight the significance of disruption to stations whose work has lost its ground state.
